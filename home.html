About

Highbanks is named for its massive 100-foot-high shale bluff towering over the Olentangy State Scenic

River. Tributary streams cutting across the bluff have created a number of deep ravines in the eastern part

of the 1,204-acre park. Ohio and Olentangy shales, often containing outstanding large concretions, are

exposed on the bluff face and sides of the ravines
